üö® EXERCISE FORM CHECKLIST

Barbell Back Squat

  • Feet shoulder-width apart, toes slightly out
  • Bar on upper traps (not neck)
  • Chest up, eyes forward
  • Break at hips AND knees simultaneously
  • Knees track over toes (don't cave inward)
  • Descend until thighs parallel (or below if mobile)
  • Drive through mid-foot
  • Squeeze glutes at top

Deadlift (Conventional)

  • Feet hip-width, bar over mid-foot
  • Grip just outside legs
  • Shoulders over or slightly in front of bar
  • Chest up, shoulder blades neutral
  • Big breath, brace core
  • Push floor away (legs first)
  • Bar stays close to body entire time
  • Lock out by thrusting hips forward
  • Control the descent (don't drop)

Bench Press

  • Lie flat, feet firmly planted
  • Grip slightly wider than shoulders
  • Shoulder blades squeezed together
  • Slight arch in lower back
  • Lower bar to mid-chest (touch gently)
  • Elbows at 45¬∞ angle (not flared out)
  • Press up explosively
  • Lock out at top without shoulder shrug

Pull-Ups

  • Full hang (arms straight) at bottom
  • Engage lats before pulling
  • Pull chest to bar (not chin)
  • Elbows drive down and back
  • Control the descent
  • No excessive swinging/kipping

Bulgarian Split Squat

  • Rear foot elevated on bench
  • Front foot far enough forward (shin vertical at bottom)
  • Torso upright
  • Lower straight down (not forward)
  • Rear knee nearly touches ground
  • Drive through front heel
  • Don't let front knee cave inward

‚ö†Ô∏è SAFETY & WARNING SIGNS

Stop Immediately If You Experience:

  • ‚ùå Sharp, sudden pain (different from muscle burn)
  • ‚ùå Joint pain that worsens during movement
  • ‚ùå Dizziness or lightheadedness
  • ‚ùå Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • ‚ùå Numbness or tingling in limbs
  • ‚ùå Extreme nausea

Seek Medical Advice If:

  • Pain persists beyond normal muscle soreness (72+ hours)
  • Swelling in joints
  • Limited range of motion after injury
  • Any concerns about exercise safety

When You Don't Feel Like Training

Use the 10-Minute Rule:

  1. Tell yourself you'll just do the warm-up.
  2. After 10 minutes, reassess.
  3. 90% of the time, you'll continue.
  4. If truly exhausted, take a rest day.
  5. One missed workout isn't failure ‚Äî get back on track tomorrow.

Remember: Motivation follows action. Discipline > Motivation. Your future self is counting on your present self.

Dealing with Plateaus

If Weight Loss Stalls (2+ weeks):

  1. Reduce daily calories by 100-200
  2. Add 10 minutes to cardio sessions
  3. Increase daily step count by 2,000
  4. Verify you're tracking food accurately
  5. Check sleep quality
  6. Reduce alcohol consumption

If Strength Stalls (3+ weeks):

  1. Ensure eating enough protein (150-180g daily)
  2. Check recovery
  3. Try a deload week (reduce weight by 20%)
  4. Verify progressive overload
  5. Change rep ranges temporarily
  6. Add an extra rest day if needed
